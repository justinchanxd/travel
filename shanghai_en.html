Shanghai 2017

Before going to Shanghai, my first impression is a flourishing city. And many of my friends have said that ShangHai and Hong Kong really look alike. After visiting, I really agree what others say. But I also found the ancient face of this beautiful city.

The famous nightview at The Bund

One side of the river are all the commercial centers as shown. In the opposite side, it is a series of old international architectures. The river in the middle separates the old days and modern Shanghai. (just like this paragraph.)

Embassies of different countries built 100 years ago

Walking further to the ancient Shanghai, I came to QiBao Town, was said the view thousands years away from now, which is the beautiful town in the background of this page. Shops there sell some souvenirs and traditional food.

A fine stone carving on the bridge

With all the modern and anicent acrchitectures, Shanghai have found a balance. Merging then and now, create a whole new Shanghai. The red pavilion says it all. Regardless the exhibit inside, the building itself is the biggest artwork.

A huge pavilion called "Oriental Crown", construction completed in the 2010 Shanghai Expo
